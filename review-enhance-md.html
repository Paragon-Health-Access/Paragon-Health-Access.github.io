Overview

Enhance MD stands out in the 2026 GLP-1 telehealth market for one primary reason — physician leadership at every step. While many platforms use algorithm-driven intake to minimize human involvement, Enhance MD keeps licensed physicians at the center of every prescription decision. This approach takes slightly longer than fully automated platforms but results in more personalized treatment protocols and comprehensive monitoring.

Their coverage of both GLP-1 (semaglutide) and GIP-GLP-1 (tirzepatide-class) medications gives patients access to the full spectrum of currently available weight loss pharmacotherapy — not just one medication class. Comprehensive lab testing is included, which is both a clinical best practice and a differentiator in a market where many competitors skip labs entirely to reduce costs.

Medication Options

Enhance MD offers both primary classes of GLP-1 weight loss medication:

  • GLP-1 medications — semaglutide-class treatments targeting the GLP-1 receptor to reduce appetite and slow gastric emptying
  • GIP-GLP-1 medications — dual-receptor agonists targeting both GIP and GLP-1 receptors, associated with higher average weight loss in clinical trials

Having both options available allows the treating physician to match medication to your specific metabolic profile rather than defaulting to one size fits all. This is clinically meaningful and reflects a higher standard of care than single-medication platforms.

Lab Testing & Medical Supervision

Comprehensive lab testing is included in the Enhance MD program. This is a significant differentiator — baseline labs allow the physician to assess metabolic health, identify contraindications, and establish monitoring benchmarks before prescribing. Many competing platforms skip labs entirely to reduce friction, which is a clinical shortcut that Enhance MD does not take.

Every prescription is reviewed and authorized by a licensed physician — not an algorithm, not a nurse practitioner working from a checklist. This standard of care is particularly important for GLP-1 medications which have known contraindications including personal or family history of medullary thyroid carcinoma and pancreatitis.
